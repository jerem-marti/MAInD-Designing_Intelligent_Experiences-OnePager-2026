Phase 1 — Design System & Visual Concept

The visual language of WeMatch.

A quiet, confident editorial system — WeRoad-adjacent, not WeRoad. It keeps the load-bearing brand signals (the coral, the typographic family, the trip-card object, the human warmth) and builds its own restraint around them: a warm-cream field, a deliberate type scale, colour used as rare punctuation. This page is both the system and a small portfolio of it.

01

Colour

A neutral base, one accent, two function-only hues — that's the whole palette. The page lives on warm cream, never white; every grey is warm. The coral is WeRoad's, but where WeRoad floods it, WeMatch reserves it. The two functional hues touch one thing only — the star glyphs in review cards — and are muted on purpose so they never read as "tech product".

Typography

General Sans carries the whole page — display and body, inside WeRoad's typographic family. One family, two weights: display sets in 500/600, body in 400. A page this restrained earns more from typographic discipline than from a second sans serif beside it. Display sizes flex with viewport; body and captions stay fixed so they don't drift. Spacing

A 4px-based scale that jumps wide at the top on purpose. The brief asks for more whitespace than a designer expects — so the scale gives it room to: the Hook needs to sit in a large empty field, and sections need to breathe apart. Small steps for component interiors, large steps for page rhythm.

Motion

Calm timings, soft easings — nothing snappy or techy. Motion serves the story; the durations climb from quick hovers to the deliberate pace of scroll-driven reveals. Hover a cell to feel each preset. All four collapse to ~instant under prefers-reduced-motion — the tokens file handles that.

06

Sofia — the character

Direction B. Sofia is the narrative spine — a character can carry her locked emotional arc where photography could only decorate around it. Produced as curated, post-processed AI illustration (a sanctioned craft choice), held consistent by a fixed identity + style prompt with only the pose changing. The assets are raster, not SVG — the soft-shaded style that reads well does not vectorise cleanly; see the flag at the foot of this page.

Identity reference
Hesitating · §02
Curious · §03
Surprised · 3b–3c
Connected · transition
Settled · §04
  • Hairstyle — dark hair in a low, slightly undone bun; reads as a clean silhouette at any size, signals competent traveller not influencer.
  • Wardrobe — one outfit across the whole page: oatmeal knit sweater, sleeves pushed up, dark trousers. The oatmeal sits inside the palette so she never fights it.
  • The phone — always in hand, its glow her single coral touchpoint. In the final state the screen is off — that absence is the arc closing.
  • Emotion through posture first — spine, shoulders, how drawn-in the arms are. The face confirms what the body already says; keeps her readable small and in motion.
07

Component prototypes

Raw HTML/CSS, no framework, built entirely on the tokens above — real text from the script, no placeholders. Each is composed editorially from the start: asymmetry, whitespace, type doing the work imagery would otherwise have to. These are what Phase 2 implements; each frame below is its own file in design/components/.

The Hook

components/hook.html

One declarative line in a large empty field, set low and left. The single accent word — "who" — carries the only coral. Nothing competes with it.

Review card

components/review-card.html

One component, three uses: the polarised §02 pair (the contrast is the argument) and the §04 closing card signed Sofia — the visual rhyme is how the page closes its loop.

Match card

components/match-card.html

Three editorial cards, score foregrounded. The score is the WeMatch artifact; the reason line in plain language is its signature. Detached from the team's Figma product card on purpose — this page tells the story above the product, not a screenshot of it.

Journey opener

The non-negotiable §03 opening: the reframe (before/after of the booking logic) then Sofia meeting WeMatch in the scene — where the brand is named for the first time.

Invitation / CTA

Craft caption
